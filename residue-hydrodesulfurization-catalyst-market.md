<p><p>The Residue Hydrodesulfurization (HDS) catalyst market is primarily divided into two types: supported catalysts and dispersible catalysts. Supported catalysts consist of active metal particles on carriers, enhancing their stability and efficiency in sulfur removal from heavy residues. In contrast, dispersible catalysts are finely dispersed metal particles, offering high activity and quick reaction rates. Both types play crucial roles in meeting stringent environmental regulations by effectively reducing sulfur content in fuels, ultimately contributing to cleaner energy production.</p></p>
