<p><p>Ceramic-based membrane filtration systems encompass three main types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration uses pores typically larger than 0.1 microns to remove suspended solids and bacteria, making it suitable for water treatment. Ultrafiltration features smaller pores for effective virus and dissolved macromolecule removal. Nanofiltration bridges the gap, targeting divalent salts and larger organic molecules while allowing monovalent ions to pass. Together, these systems offer versatile solutions across various industries, including water purification, food processing, and wastewater treatment.</p></p>
