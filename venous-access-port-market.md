<p><strong>The Venous Access Port Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>PICC</li><li>Centrally Inserted Catheters (non-tunnelled and tunnelled)</li><li>Implanted Ports</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The venous access port market includes various types that facilitate long-term medication delivery. Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC) are inserted through peripheral veins, offering limited duration use. Centrally Inserted Catheters can be non-tunnelled for short-term access or tunnelled for long-term use, providing stability and reduced infection risk. Implanted ports are subcutaneously placed for intermittent access, minimizing trauma. Additionally, "Others" may encompass specialized devices catering to unique patient needs, enhancing treatment options in diverse clinical settings.</p></p>
