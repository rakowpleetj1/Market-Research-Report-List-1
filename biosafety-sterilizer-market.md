<p><p>The biosafety sterilizer market encompasses various levels of containment based on the BioSafety Levels (BSL). BSL-1 includes basic sterilization for handling low-risk agents. BSL-2 is tailored for moderate-risk pathogens, requiring enhanced sterilization measures. BSL-3 deals with high-risk organisms that can cause serious diseases, demanding stringent sterilization protocols. BSL-4, the highest level, addresses extreme hazards and necessitates rigorous sterilization techniques to prevent contamination and ensure safety. Each category reflects increasing safety requirements and technological sophistication in sterilization methods.</p></p>
