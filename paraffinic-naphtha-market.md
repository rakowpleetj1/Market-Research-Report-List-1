<p><p>The paraffinic naphtha market consists of two main types: straight run naphtha and secondary processing naphtha. Straight run naphtha is derived directly from crude oil distillation, characterized by its light hydrocarbon composition, making it suitable for gasoline blending and petrochemical feedstocks. In contrast, secondary processing naphtha is produced through additional refining processes such as catalytic cracking and reforming, enhancing its quality and yield for various applications, including higher-value chemical intermediates and specialty products. Both types are integral to the energy and chemical sectors.</p></p>
